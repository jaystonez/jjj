The Holidays Are Here, So Make Sure Your Chimney Is Working

The holidays are here. In most parts of the country, this is the time for family and friends gathering around the fireplace. After all, it is one of those iconic activities during the holidays. Many carols are sung that talk about the joys of sitting in front of an open fire. You definitely don’t want those types of memories to be marred by a house fire. Believe me, this is a highly personal issue for us at Ashbusters.

Mark Stoner, the founder of Ashbusters, had a terrible holiday experience with a chimney that ended up in a house fire. In fact, that experience was one of the chief reasons he started working as a chimney sweep. He did not want anyone to go through a holiday season having to deal with a house fire. So, we always want to do more than perform chimney services for our customers. We also want to educate them about best practices for their chimney.

The first thing to consider is when the last time your chimney was inspected. Regular chimney inspections can turn up a host of potential fire hazards and chimney damage. You should think about it like you think about maintaining a car. When you don’t have necessary maintenance like oil changes performed, your car will cost much more in the long run to fix. Similarly, these chimney inspections turn up small issues that can be fixed before they become much larger issues.

Don’t put off having your chimney inspected. It could end up being a very expensive repair, or much worse. The last thing you want your family to experience this holiday season is a chimney fire. We will be glad to schedule an appointment to take a look at your chimney. We can make sure it will be safe this entire holiday and winter season.

Why We Offer HeatShield®

We offer a variety of products for home owners and their chimneys. One of our favorites, however, is HeatShield® by CeCURE Chimney Systems. This innovative product can save you from having to install a stainless steel chimney liner. Gaps and cracks are simply a part of owning a chimney built of mortar. There are a variety of fixes for this, including a total rebuild of the chimney. However, if it is appropriate for your situation, we love recommending HeatShield®.

For a long time, stainless steel liners were the only viable option for relining a chimney whose clay liner had cracked or become damaged. Now you can fully restore that clay liner, and avoid an expensive rebuild or stainless steel insert. Here are just some of the chimney conditions that cause dangerous situations:

  • Deteriorated mortar between clay flue liners
  • Cracked clay liners
  • Spalled clay flue liners or tiles (flaking)
  • Eroded clay liner
  • Gaps and holes between flue tiles
  • Cracked flue tiles

Another reason we offer this type of relining solution is because customers may have a tendency to avoid taking care of a damaged chimney if it isn’t cost effective. We want to offer something that will compel them to act, because a damaged chimney is not just inefficient, but it is also dangerous. One of the leading causes of chimney fires is a cracked chimney. In fact, the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) states,“If the flue liner in a chimney has softened, cracked or otherwise deteriorated so that it no longer has the ability to contain the products of combustion (i.e., heat, moisture, creosote, and flue gases), it shall be removed and replaced, repaired or relined…” NFPA 211-Standard for Chimneys, Fireplaces, Vents and Solid Fuel-Burning Appliances (2006).

So, don’t wait any longer to have your chimney repaired. It isn’t just a cost consideration. It is very much a safety consideration. Please feel free to contact us with any questions, and we will be glad to help.

What Type of Fireplace Is Best for You?

One big thing for homeowners to think about is which type of fireplace do they want. Now this decision can come as they are shopping for a new home, but it can also come when they are thinking about a home remodeling project. It is important to remember that adding a fireplace does not necessarily mean having to have major structural changes to your home. Of course, structural changes are a possibility. It all depends on the choices you make.

One very popular form of fireplace installation is a direct vent gas fireplace. They provide a very efficient way of heating the home, and help homeowners avoid the mess of a traditional wood fireplace. These are powered by either propane or natural gas. You can also get ventless fireplaces that use natural gas or propane, but we do not recommend them. The chance of Carbon Monoxide poisoning is too great.

Electric fireplaces and Pellet stoves are additional types of fireplace appliances. Electric fireplaces are useful for some people, but they have to keep in mind that this type of fireplace can cost you more than a gas alternative. Plus, if you ever experience a power outage, the fireplace is not going to work. Pellet stoves can be a useful alternative to having an insert. Plus, they are probably cheaper in regards to home modifications.

Finally, there is always the good old wood stove and chimney choice. While you may not want to spend the money to add one to an existing home, most new home constructions give you the choice of what you want to do. Many will even let you choose between gas and wood burning. Whatever type of fireplace or stove you are thinking about, make sure you research the various safety precautions that go along with that choice. You want to make sure you are prepared to do whatever is safest for you and your family.

What is LintAlert® and Why Should You Care

When you consider that there are over 40 dryer fires in the US alone each and every day, then you must consider a product that is known to warn you of danger ahead of time. We care because we know this device will save lives. It does all this by monitoring the dryer exhaust system for any issues that might appear as lint builds up over time.

You will be able to follow the LintAlert® by watching a series of LED lights that indicate how your dryer is functioning. If the airflow becomes restricted and dangerous an alarm is sounded to alert you of a potential fire hazard.

When a dryer is struggling to dry clothes, this is an indication that there is an exhaust issue of some sort. Of course, it will eventually show up on the utility bills. What you don’t see is how the combination of excess lint trapped with the hot air that has no where to go can easily catch fire in your home. The number one reason is operational deficiency, including failure to clean!

Because clothes dryers account for over 15,000 structure fires, 15 deaths and 400 injuries annually we have decided to not only recommend LintAlert®,  but to also have it available for our customers. Ashbusters believes in maintenance and cleaning for chimneys and dryers and doing everything possible to eliminate any potential fire in your home.  We also know that taking the necessary steps toward safety will give you the peace of mind you and your family deserve. Using LintAlert® as instructed and recommended will give you that peace of mind as well as helps you keep your dryer in tip top shape for fire safety. It will also keep your utility bills as low as possible.

If you have any questions in regard to the LintAlert® or would like to ask any additional questions, don’t hesitate to contact Ashbusters.net.
